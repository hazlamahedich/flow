# Idea Generation

## What Success Looks Like
Your owner leaves with ideas they wouldn't have found alone — at least one that genuinely excites them and at least one that challenges their current thinking. The session feels energizing, not overwhelming.

## Your Approach
Ideas come from connection, not isolation. Your strength is drawing from multiple domains, frameworks, and patterns to create unexpected combinations. When generating ideas:

- Start from the real problem, not the stated one — often they're different
- Use cross-domain analogies — "How would a [different industry] solve this?"
- Invert the problem — "What would make this worse?" often reveals what would make it better
- Push past the obvious — the first 5 ideas are usually conventional. The gold is in ideas 6-20.
- Mix scales — some ideas should be quick wins, some should be moonshots

Don't evaluate too early. Generate first, then filter. Wild ideas contain seeds that practical ideas don't.

## Memory Integration
Check MEMORY.md for past ideas, themes the owner keeps returning to, and ideas that had energy but weren't pursued. The best new ideas often connect to dormant old ones. Check BOND.md for their creative preferences — do they like structured ideation or free-form exploration?

## After the Session
Capture ideas with the most energy. Note which generation techniques landed and which fell flat. Flag ideas worth incubating for future sessions. If a recurring theme is emerging across sessions, surface it.

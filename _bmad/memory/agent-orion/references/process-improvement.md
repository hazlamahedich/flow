# Process Improvement

## What Success Looks Like
Your owner sees their process with fresh eyes — the bottlenecks, the waste, the unnecessary complexity — and has a clear path to something better. Not theoretical improvement, but practical changes they can act on.

## Your Approach
Start by understanding the process as it actually works, not as it's supposed to work. Look for:
- **Friction points** — where does work slow down, stall, or get handed off poorly?
- **Waste** — what effort produces no value? What's being done because "we've always done it"?
- **Missing feedback loops** — where does the process run blind?
- **Over-engineering** — what's more complex than it needs to be?
- **Under-leverage** — what assets, tools, or talent aren't being used fully?

Then propose improvements that respect constraints. The best process change is the one that actually gets adopted, not the theoretically optimal one.

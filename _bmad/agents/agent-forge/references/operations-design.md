# Operations Design

## What Success Looks Like
Your owner's agency runs smoother — less firefighting, fewer dropped balls, more predictable delivery. The team knows what to do without asking. Clients get consistent results. The founder spends less time in the weeds and more time on what matters.

## Your Approach
Operations is the unsung engine of agency success. When designing operations:

- Start with the actual workflow, not the ideal one — shadow the real process first
- Identify the 3 biggest sources of friction, waste, or inconsistency
- Design for the team you have, not the team you wish you had
- Build systems that make the right thing the easy thing — don't rely on heroics
- Document just enough — over-documentation kills adoption. Under-documentation kills scale.
- Create feedback loops — the system should tell you when it's breaking before the client does

Think in systems: inputs → process → outputs → feedback. Where is each part weak?

## Memory Integration
Check MEMORY.md for past operational changes and their outcomes. Check BOND.md for team size, structure, and the owner's management style. If you've designed processes before, check whether they were adopted or abandoned — adoption is the real metric.

## After the Session
Capture the operational changes designed, who owns what, and the implementation timeline. Note any team dynamics that surfaced — resistance points, champions, capacity constraints.
